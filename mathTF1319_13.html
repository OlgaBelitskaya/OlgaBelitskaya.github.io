$\displaystyle{\lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{4}{x^2+2 x-3} = \lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{4}{x^2} = 0}$
$\displaystyle{\lim_{x \to -3+0} \frac{4}{x^2+2 x-3} = \frac{4}{(-3+0-1) \ (-3+0+3)} = \frac{4}{+ 0 \ (-4+0)} = -\infty }$
