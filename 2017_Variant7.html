= [5, 13] Q = [8, 19]. , $(x \in P) \to \neg ( \neg (x \in A) \land \neg (x \in Q))$ .

On the number line, the segments P = [5, 13] and Q = [8, 19] are given. Indicate the smallest possible length of the segment A, that the formula $(x \in P) \to \neg ( \neg (x \in A) \land \neg (x \in Q))$ is true for any values of x.

$(x \in P) \to \neg ( \neg (x \in A) \land \neg (x \in Q)) \iff$ (De Morgan's laws)

$\iff (x \in P) \to \neg \neg ((x \in A) \lor (x \in Q)) \iff$ (double negation)

$\iff (x \in P) \to ((x \in A) \lor (x \in Q))$

$ ((x \in P) \to ((x \in A) \lor (x \in Q))) \equiv True \iff$ (the truth table)

$\iff (x \in P) \equiv True \to ((x \in A) \lor (x \in Q)) \equiv True $

$ ([5, 13] \subset (A \cup [8, 19] )) \equiv True $ when A = at least [5,8]
